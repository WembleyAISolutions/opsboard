# Autonomy Permission Tiers (Future)

AI agents can be granted different autonomy levels.

### Tier 0 — Signal Only
AI emits signals but does not execute actions.

### Tier 1 — Suggestion
AI proposes actions but requires human approval.

### Tier 2 — Limited Execution
AI can execute low-risk tasks autonomously.

### Tier 3 — Domain Autonomy
AI executes defined work within clear boundaries and reports results.

OpsBoard remains the **human oversight interface**.

# Design Philosophy

OpsBoard intentionally avoids:

- chat interfaces
- kanban boards
- complex workflow engines
- heavy dashboards

Instead, it focuses on:

- clarity
- calm interface
- signal visibility
- explicit human control
- gradual AI autonomy

The system acts as a **lightweight operational gateway between human judgment and AI execution**.

---

# Summary

OpsBoard provides a stable governance model for human-AI collaboration.

The system ensures:

- important work is visible
- priorities are correct
- decisions remain explicit
- autonomy grows responsibly

OpsBoard is designed as a **Human–AI Work Control Surface**.

Its primary role is to ensure that:

- important AI signals are surfaced
- human attention is directed correctly
- approvals are explicit
- work progress remains visible
- autonomy grows safely over time

The system follows a **signal-first governance model** before enabling broader AI autonomy.

---

# Core Principles

OpsBoard is guided by four foundational principles.

## 1. Signal First

All AI activity enters OpsBoard through **signals**, not tasks.

Signals represent:

- status updates
- progress changes
- alerts
- human decisions required
- incoming information

Signals ensure **nothing important is missed**.

---

## 2. Priority Before Automation

Correct prioritization is more important than automation.

The system must first determine:

- what requires attention
- what can wait
- what requires human decision

Only after priorities are stable should automation expand.

---

## 3. Human Oversight Before Broad Autonomy

AI systems must demonstrate reliability before gaining more autonomy.

OpsBoard ensures:

- approvals are explicit
- human intervention is possible
- signals are visible before execution expands

This prevents automation from creating operational risk.

---

## 4. Autonomy Grows Through Demonstrated Completion

AI autonomy should increase **gradually based on proven performance**.

As AI systems complete work reliably over time, they can be granted expanded permissions.

OpsBoard records signals and completions that form the basis for this trust.
